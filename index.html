Tabla Pro — Basics for Kids

A 20-week beginner curriculum in 5 levels, built the traditional way: bols → bol-groups → taals. Verified video lessons in one place, everything notated in English with built-in play-along, and a playable tabla — all you need to teach any kid the basics.

How to use: one card = one week, grouped into 5 levels. Open the week you are teaching and share its videos and bol-groups as that week's reference. Each card has the goal, concepts, a class plan, measurable drills, homework and a teacher tip. Levels 1–4 (weeks 1–16) are the core "basics" course; Level 5 is the optional stretch for fast learners.

Reading the notation: beats run left to right; each box of beats is one vibhag (group). + = sam (beat 1 — clap), 👏 = tali (clap), 〰️ = khali (wave the hand, play quiet), – = rest (silent beat). Press ▶ to hear any theka — the beats light up as they play, and you can change the speed. Switch the dropdown to 🎻 Lehra only and the tabla drops out, leaving the melodic loop a real accompanist plays to: one cycle of melody, over and over, so the student always knows where sam is. That is how tabla is actually practised — to a lehra, not a metronome. The 6 course taals come first in teaching order; below them is the full reference of classical taals.
The tabla alphabet. Every rhythm is spoken before it is played (padhant). Learn to say each bol, then to play it. Press ▶ on any row to hear it. Dayan = right (treble) drum, Bayan = left (bass) drum.

Bols are letters. Bol-groups (varnas) are words. Taals are sentences. This is the rung between single strokes and full thekas — drill these little groups until the hands own them, and a taal later assembles from pieces the child already plays instead of being memorised beat by beat. Press ▶ to hear any group; the builds into links show exactly where that group reappears inside a real taal.
The streak is the lesson. Ten minutes a day beats an hour on Sunday — little hands build habits, not stamina. Tap the button after each practice and watch the squares fill up. Saved on this device only; nothing is uploaded anywhere.

The teacher's guide. The things a curriculum of videos and notation does not cover, but that decide whether a child actually progresses: the daily practice shape, what to do when something sounds wrong, how to count a cycle on the hand, and what to tell parents.
The practice tabla. Tap the drums — each zone makes its real stroke: the edge (chaat) plays Na, the middle (maidan) plays Tin, the black spot (syahi) plays Tun; on the bass drum the head plays Ge and the syahi plays Ka.
